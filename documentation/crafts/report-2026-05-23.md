## Bottom line
**No clean deterministic +EV craft in the current data.** The exactly-priceable
plays are unprofitable or unreliable this league; the genuinely profitable plays
(guaranteed-mod essence crafting) need finished-item valuation that PoE2 market
data doesn't support yet. Revisit next patch / as item-market liquidity thickens.

## 1. Shard ↔ orb arbitrage (deterministic, 10 shards = 1 orb) — all negative
Combining shards *destroys* value right now (shards are priced richer than the orb):

| Combine | 10× shard cost | Orb value | EV |
|---|--:|--:|--:|
| Regal Orb | 10.0 | 0.36 | **−96%** |
| Orb of Transmutation | 14.9 | 0.44 | **−97%** |
| Orb of Chance | 50.0 | 5.73 | **−89%** |
| Artificer's Orb | 173.3 | 5.30 | **−97%** |

Takeaway: don't combine shards — sell them, or buy the whole orb. (Shard prices are
thin and likely noisy, but the direction is clear.)

## 2. Tier upgrades (essence/rune Lesser→normal→Greater) — NOT an arb
PoE2 tier upgrades go through the **Reforging Bench as a low-odds reforge (~1–2%
upgrade chance), not a guaranteed 3:1 recipe** — so "buy 3 Lesser, upgrade, sell
higher" is a gamble, not arbitrage. Excluded from EV. (Essence tier prices are also
too thin to trust: e.g. a "Lesser Essence of Abrasion" prices *above* its normal —
illiquidity, not opportunity.)

## 3. Demand signal — highest-priced essences (where mod demand is)

finished-item / listing data is available per base+mod. Path of Crafting (MIT) gives

third input — expected to improve as the league/patch matures. Re-run the skill then.
